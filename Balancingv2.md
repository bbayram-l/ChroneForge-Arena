Let's dive into the math. Designing these curves is where your game goes from feeling like a spreadsheet calculator to a tense, risk-reward engine.The most common trap in strategy game design is relying too heavily on linear math. Linear math is predictable, and predictability kills the "emergent mechanical failure" pillar you are aiming for. We want curves that create a false sense of security, followed by a sharp "danger zone."Here is how we can balance your three primary curves: Heat, Paradox, and Diminishing Returns.🔥 1. Heat Overload: The "Meltdown Cliff"Your original formula is linear:$OverheatPenalty = 1 - \frac{ExcessHeat}{MaxHeat}$The Problem: If a player has 10% excess heat, they lose 10% efficiency. If they have 50%, they lose 50%. It’s too smooth. There is no psychological "cliff" that makes the Thermal Overdrive archetype feel dangerous.The Solution: Use an exponential or quadratic curve. This allows mechs to run slightly hot with minimal penalty, but severely punishes them if they ignore the heat creep.Proposed Formula (Quadratic Penalty):$$OverheatPenalty = \max\left(0.1, 1 - \left( \frac{ExcessHeat}{MaxHeat} \right)^2 \right)$$(Note: I added a max() floor of 0.1 so the mech doesn't completely stop functioning, it just crawls to a near-halt).Excess Heat %Linear Penalty (Old)Quadratic Penalty (New)Player Experience10%0.90x efficiency0.99x efficiency"I'm running a little hot, but I'm fine."30%0.70x efficiency0.91x efficiency"Warning lights are blinking."60%0.40x efficiency0.64x efficiency"Systems failing, I am losing DPS fast!"90%0.10x efficiency0.19x efficiency"Complete structural lockdown."⏳ 2. Paradox Overload: The "Push Your Luck" CurveYour original formula for a timeline collapse:$OverloadChance = (Paradox - 100) \times 0.02$The Problem: This means at 101 Paradox, there is a 2% chance to overload. At 150 Paradox, you hit exactly 100% chance. The window between "safe" and "guaranteed destruction" is a flat 50-point line.The Solution: An S-Curve (Logistic Function) or an Exponential CDF. This makes breaching the 100-limit feel like rolling a loaded die.
